The Inclusive Or

In mathematics, the word "or" is understood to be inclusive; "Yes or No" is true if Yes is true, if No is true, and if Yes and No are both true.

The use of "or" is associated with Boolean logic and binary assertions of true or false.

Nevertheless, the assumed inclusive nature of "or" demonstrates that truthfullness may lie in being not one or the other but both (or all).

The inclusive "or" envelops an entirety with a small word that in other contexts presupposes an exclusionary choice.
